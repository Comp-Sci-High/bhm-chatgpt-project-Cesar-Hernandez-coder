Celebrating Black History Month

Chadwick Boseman

Chadwick Aaron Boseman was an American actor, writer, and director who portrayed Black historical figures and the superhero Black Panther. He was known for his roles as Jackie Robinson in 42 and James Brown in Get on Up, and also starred as Thurgood Marshall in the 2017 film Marshall. Boseman's career spanned two decades and earned him an Academy Award nomination, a Golden Globe, and a Primetime Emmy Award.

Martin Luther King Jr

Martin Luther King Jr. was an American Baptist minister, activist, and political philosopher who was one of the most prominent leaders in the civil rights movement from 1955 until his assassination in 1968.

Samuel L. Jackson

Samuel Leroy Jackson is an American actor known for his commanding presence, distinctive voice, and versatility. He's the highest-grossing actor of all time, with his films earning more than $27 billion worldwide. Jackson has played iconic roles like Jules Winnfield in Pulp Fiction, a reformed assassin with a memorable goatee and biting wit. In Django Unchained, he portrayed Stephen Warren, an enslaved man brainwashed into servitude.
