Singers

Michael Jackson

Michael Joseph Jackson was an American singer, songwriter, dancer, and philanthropist. Dubbed the "King of Pop", he is regarded as one of the most significant figures of the 20th century. Over a four-decade career, his world record music achievements broke racial barriers in America and made him a global figure.

Louis Armstrong

Louis Daniel Armstrong, nicknamed “Satchmo”, was a trumpeter and vocalist who helped shape jazz into an art form and introduce it to the world. Considered a leading figure in jazz history, his career spanned five decades and multiple jazz eras. Armstrong's vocal improvisations and swing style loosened up the more formal style of his contemporaries.

Aretha Franklin

Aretha Louise Franklin was an American singer, songwriter, and pianist who defined the 1960s soul music era and earned the title "Queen of Soul". Rolling Stone magazine named her the greatest singer of all time twice. Franklin was known for her four-octave vocal range and was involved in the fight for civil and women's rights, financially supporting civil rights groups and performing at protests and benefits.
